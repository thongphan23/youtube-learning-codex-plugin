# Socratic Worldview Contract

The old `/youtube-learn` workflow should not produce vague "insights." A worldview must become a usable mental object.

## Required Shape

For each worldview, answer:

1. **Question**: What question does this worldview answer?
2. **Evidence**: What exactly did the speaker say or do?
3. **Essence**: What is it really?
4. **Boundary**: What is it not?
5. **Confusion**: What do people usually confuse it with?
6. **Mechanism**: Why does it work or matter?
7. **Signs**: What are its real-life expressions?
8. **Types**: Are there practical subtypes?
9. **Use**: How should the learner use it?
10. **Payoff**: What benefit does it create?
11. **Risk**: What mistake does it prevent?
12. **Next action**: What is the smallest thing the learner can do?

## Writing Style

Use Socratic questions, but answer them. Do not leave the learner with homework only.

Good:

```text
Question: When does a tool stop being a tool?
Answer: When it can choose goals, preserve itself, use other tools, and act before you approve each step.
```

Bad:

```text
This is about control and safety.
```

## Concreteness Test

A worldview passes only if the user can:

- Recognize it in another speaker.
- Apply it to one decision this week.
- Explain its wrong version.
- Name one situation where it fails.
- Turn it into a note, product rule, or content angle.
